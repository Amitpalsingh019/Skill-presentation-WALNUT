My Journey as an International Student Entrepreneur

Four years ago, as an international student, I arrived with dreams and aspirations of making a mark in a foreign land. One thing I noticed was the lack of availability of familiar groceries from my home country. Recognizing this gap in the market, I saw an opportunity to start a grocery store catering to international students like myself.

With determination and relentless effort, I embarked on the journey of entrepreneurship. In the first year, I focused on understanding the market, conducting thorough research, and developing a solid business plan. I utilized my academic knowledge, sought guidance from mentors, and attended business workshops to equip myself with the necessary skills.

By the second year, I secured funding through a combination of personal savings, loans, and grants available to student entrepreneurs. With the financial backing, I acquired a suitable retail space and began sourcing products from local and international suppliers.

The third year marked the grand opening of my grocery store. Despite facing challenges such as navigating regulations, building a customer base, and managing operations, I remained resilient. I leveraged digital marketing, word-of-mouth referrals, and community engagement to attract customers.
